# The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a fundamental cybersecurity model that ensures information security through three principles:

- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability
---
# 1. Confidentiality
## Explanation

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized users.

It protects data from unauthorized disclosure.

## Examples:

- Password protection
- Encryption
- Access control systems
---
## Advantages
- Protects private information
- Prevents data leaks
- Enhances user trust
---
## Limitations
- Complex access management
- Encryption may reduce speed
- Human errors can expose data
- Threats to Confidentiality
- Packet sniffing
- Password theft
- Hacking
- Accidental data sharing
---
# 2. Integrity
## Explanation

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unmodified.

Data should not be altered by unauthorized users.

## Examples:

- Hashing
- Digital signatures
- Checksums
---
## Advantages
- Maintains data accuracy
- Prevents unauthorized modifications
- Improves data reliability
---
## Limitations
- Requires additional security tools
- Hashing consumes processing power
- Insider threats may still exist
- Threats to Integrity
- Malware attacks
- Unauthorized editing
- Data corruption during transmission
---
# 3. Availability
## Explanation

Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible whenever authorized users need them.

Systems should remain operational without interruptions.

## Examples:

- Backup systems
- Cloud storage
- Redundant servers
---
## Advantages
- Continuous access to services
- Reduces downtime
- Supports business operations
---
## Limitations
- Expensive infrastructure
- Maintenance costs
- Vulnerable to DoS attacks
- Threats to Availability
- Hardware failure
- Power outages
- Denial of Service (DoS) attacks
- Natural disasters
